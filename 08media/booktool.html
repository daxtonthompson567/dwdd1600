Famous books

To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee
Published in 1960, this Pulitzer Prize-winning novel explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion in the American South during the 1930s. The story follows young Scout Finch and her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, as they confront prejudice and inequality in their community.
1984" by George Orwell
Written in 1949, this dystopian novel delves into the dangers of totalitarianism, surveillance, and propaganda. Set in a future society under the rule of Big Brother, it follows Winston Smith, a man who begins to question the oppressive regime controlling every aspect of life.
The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald
First published in 1925, this novel is set in the Jazz Age of the 1920s and examines themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream. It tells the story of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire, and his obsession with rekindling a lost romance with Daisy Buchanan.
